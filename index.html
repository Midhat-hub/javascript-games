F = ma • Elastic walls

What you see (quick):

Pressing an arrow key applies a constant force in that direction. The ball accelerates according to the force and mass (bigger mass → smaller acceleration). When the ball hits a wall it bounces off elastically (no energy lost in this demo).

Newton's Laws — short & sweet

  1. First law — Inertia: An object stays at rest or moves at constant velocity unless an external force acts on it.
    In the game: If you don't press any keys (no force), the ball keeps moving at the same velocity.
  2. Second law — F = ma: The net force on an object equals mass × acceleration. So acceleration = force / mass.
    In the game: Pressing an arrow key applies force. The slider for mass changes how strongly that force accelerates the ball.
  3. Third law — Action & reaction: Forces come in equal and opposite pairs.
    In the game: When the ball hits a wall the wall applies an impulse that reverses the ball's velocity (equal/opposite effect). The collision is modeled as an elastic bounce.

Tip: Try heavy mass + big force, then light mass + same force — feel the difference in acceleration. Science is tactile, and this is your remote-control lab.
